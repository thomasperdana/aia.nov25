Core Principle: AI = Assistant, NOT Substitute

  • Assists with mechanics of study
  • Helps with language, context, structure
  • Processes information quickly
  • Guides without concluding
  • Informs and explains theology

The Journey: 6 Levels of Progress

1

Starting Simple — Basic Prompt

Example:
"Bible Study on John 3:16"
Why it underperforms:
  • Vague goal → generic output
  • No method → random structure
  • No constraints → AI concludes for you
2

Adding Specificity — Clearer Intent

Improved prompt:
"Bible Study on John 3:16 (KJV). Ask me questions and do not make conclusions unless I ask."
Why it's better:
  • Names translation and passage
  • Defines AI's role (guide, not preacher)
  • Protects your agency (no unauthorized conclusions)
3

The Inductive Method

Observation

What does it say?

  • • Who speaks?
  • • To whom?
  • • Key words/verbs?
Interpretation

What does it mean?

  • • Original audience?
  • • Literary context?
  • • Word meanings?
Application

What should I do?

  • • Timeless principle
  • • Personal practice
  • • Communal practice
Inductive Prompt Template:
"Act as my guide for an Inductive Bible Study of John 3:16 (KJV). 1) Observation: Ask me only text-based questions first. 2) Interpretation: Then guide with context/meaning questions. 3) Application: Finally, ask application questions. Only ask questions; do not give conclusions unless I explicitly ask."

Tip: Add "one question at a time" to pace the session.

4

Two-AI Collaboration

User
Orchestrator
(AI 1)
Expert Guide
(AI 2)
Benefits:
  • Clear roles, fewer detours
  • Each AI has specific responsibilities
  • Stronger discipline (no premature conclusions)
  • Expert Guide remains focused solely on questioning
Scalability:
  • Swap in expert personas when needed
  • Replace Expert Guide with specialized personas
  • Deeper insights with specialized knowledge
5

Expert Persona System

Word/Text Focus:
KJV Historian & Textual Critic

1611 context, manuscript sources, translation choices

Biblical Linguist & Etymologist

Greek/Hebrew meanings + Early Modern English nuances

Literary Critic

Genre, structure, rhetorical devices, narrative flow

Theology/Context/Application:
Systematic Theologian

Doctrine, cross-references, biblical unity

Cultural Historian & Archaeologist

Ancient customs, artifacts, real-world context

Pastor & Practical Theologian

Application, devotional insights, modern relevance

Master Exegete Prompt:
"Act as a Master Biblical Scholar and KJV Exegete. Analyze [INSERT PASSAGE] weaving together: original languages + KJV choices, historical/cultural context, literary structure, doctrinal connections (with KJV cross-refs), and timeless application. Present as a single flowing commentary, not separate lists."
